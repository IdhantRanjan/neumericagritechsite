## The paperwork nobody assigns

Farm programs don't send invoices when you qualify. ARC/PLC elections,
storage facility loans, conservation cost-shares, disaster assistance —
each sits behind a form at the county office, and the enrollment window
closes whether or not anyone told you it was open.

The uncomfortable pattern: the operations that collect are usually the
ones with somebody whose job includes reading FSA notices — a bookkeeper,
an office manager, a very patient spouse. The operations that miss out are
busy farming.

A quick self-audit worth an evening:

- **Base acres:** If you have them, did you make an ARC/PLC election this
  cycle, and did anyone re-check the choice when the county numbers moved?
- **On-farm storage:** The Farm Storage Facility Loan program finances
  bins and grain-handling at rates your bank usually can't match. If
  you've priced a bin lately, price the FSFL alongside it.
- **Cover crops / no-till:** Conservation programs (EQIP, CSP, state
  cost-shares) pay for practices some operations already run unpaid.
- **A documented loss last season:** Disaster programs and the crop
  insurance you already bought interact — a loss that was under-documented
  for one can sometimes still qualify for the other.

None of this is exotic. It's a checklist against your county office's
current fact sheets — and the habit of asking "what changed this year?"
every winter.
