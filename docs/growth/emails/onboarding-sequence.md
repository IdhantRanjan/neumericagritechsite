## Email 2 — day 4

**Subject:** The 72-hour rule most farmers learn the hard way

Crop insurance requires notice of loss within 72 hours of discovering
damage. Miss it and a real claim can get denied on a technicality —
worst-case, that's the whole season's indemnity.

What to do the day damage happens: (1) call your agent — a phone call
counts, get the time and name down; (2) photograph the damage from your
phone before any cleanup, wide shots and close-ups; (3) don't destroy
evidence — leave affected areas untouched until the adjuster's seen them
or you have written permission.

Neumeric tracks this clock per claim and builds the photo evidence into a
packet your adjuster can actually use. But even if you never use us: put
your agent's number in your phone favorites today.

## Email 3 — day 10

**Subject:** What "basis" is quietly costing you

Basis — the gap between the futures price on the screen and the cash bid at
your elevator — moves independently of futures. Selling when basis is
unusually wide gives away money even when the futures price looks fine.

Three numbers worth knowing for your local market: your typical harvest
basis, your typical spring basis, and where basis is today. If today's
basis is tighter than your typical range, that's information. Wider — also
information. (This is education, not trading advice: the decisions and the
results are yours.)

Neumeric's marketing dashboard keeps your basis range next to your
breakeven and your insurance floor, so a selling decision is one screen,
not five guesses.
